Here we show first the number of repetitions in the set of publications selected. On the left the cardinality (e.g., 1 means not repetions, 2 means doplications, etc.), while the number on the right represent the number of ocurrences (e.g., 7 indicate that there are seven publications with the corresponding cardinality). In this way, the pair (3,7) represent that there are 7 papers with 3 ocurrences. There is not overlapping among the quantities, if one paper is counted in an specific cardinality, won't be counted in any of the other ones.

Secondly, we show similar information relating to the type of publication. For example, the pair (article,4) say that there are four articles.
